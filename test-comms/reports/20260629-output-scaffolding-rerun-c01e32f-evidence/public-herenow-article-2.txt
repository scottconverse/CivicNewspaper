Upcoming City Council Meeting on July 7th Includes Open Forum for Public Input
PUBLISHED: 2026-06-29T16:39:32.182175100+00:00 FORMAT: WATCH

The upcoming City Council meeting scheduled for Tuesday, July 7th, from 7 pm to 11 pm at the City Hall (350 Kimbark St., Longmont, CO) will feature an open forum where residents can engage in local governance. Source

During this meeting, council members will discuss various topics including project and program updates such as money-saving rebates and efficiency resources, which are part of the Sustainable Business Program aimed at promoting environmental stewardship, economic vitality, and social equity. Source

Residents interested in senior community engagement can also look forward to updates on programs and events at the Senior Center. Additionally, discussions will include Vision Zero projects and activities focused on improving transportation safety. The council may seek public input or feedback on these initiatives during the open forum. Source

Moreover, the city is considering expanding its language services by recording email newsletter preferences for potential Spanish translations in the future. Longmont residents can now follow the City of Longmont’s WhatsApp channel to receive news and updates in Spanish. Source

The agenda also includes regular sessions where formal business will be conducted, including ordinances (local laws) that must go through two readings before they become law. The first reading introduces an ordinance to the council for discussion and potential scheduling of a public hearing. Source
