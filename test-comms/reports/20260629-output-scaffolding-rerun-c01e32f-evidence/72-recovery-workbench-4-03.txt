Frequently Asked Questions
What are the different types of City Council meetings?
Regular Sessions
Regular Sessions are the formal business meetings of City Council and they can only take formal action at Regular Sessions. Regular Sessions, prescribed by the City&#8217;s Charter to be held at least twice a month, are usually held on the second and fourth Tuesdays of every month but are sometimes scheduled on other Tuesdays due to holidays or scheduling conflicts.  Four members of Council constitute a quorum and may conduct City business. Council conducts business at a Regular Session in one of the following manners:
&nbsp;
Ordinances (Local Laws) –
An ordinance is a type of local law. It must go through  two readings  before it becomes law.
First Reading:  The ordinance is introduced to the City Council. Council members can ask staff questions. Then the Council votes on whether to publish the ordinance and schedule a public hearing.
