Longmont City Council Voting Process and Public Participation

Published: 2026-06-30T11:15:03.711015600+00:00 Format: watch

The Longmont City Council has taken an official action to pass an ordinance related to the city's legislative process Source. This indicates that a decision was made during recent meetings regarding how laws are enacted in the city. Residents should stay informed about upcoming council meetings, as public participation opportunities may arise. For more details on how to participate, visit the Agenda Management Portal or contact the City Clerk's Office at 303-651-8649. What to watch for next: Future council meeting agendas and notices of any proposed changes to city ordinances.

Past videos are also archived on the City's YouTube channel. How Can Residents Participate in Meetings? If you want to speak at a City Council meeting, you need to sign up before the meeting starts. A sign-up sheet will be available outside the Council Chambers starting at 6:30 p.m. The City Clerk collects the list(s) right before the meeting begins at 7:00 p.m. &nbsp; Speaking Time Limit: Each speaker is allowed up to 3 minutes . Speakers may not assign their time to others. &nbsp; If you need assistance to participate in a City Council meeting, please contact the City Clerk's Office at 303-651-8649 at least five (5) days in advance of the meeting to make arrangements. What are the rules for public participation? Regular Session Meetings First Call - Public Invited to Be Heard Open to Longmont residents with a Longmont address and City of Longmont employees only. You must sign up before the meeting starts . &nbsp; Second Reading or Public Hearing Items Anyone can speak on these topics. You're encouraged to sign up, but sign-up is not required . &nbsp; Final Call - Public Invited to Be Heard Anyone can speak at the end of the meeting. No sign-up is needed . &nbsp; Study Session Meetings Public Invited to Be Heard Anyone can speak. You must sign up before the meeting starts . How does the legislative process begin? The laws of the City of Longmont are enacted when an ordinance is passed by City Council. &nbsp; Essentially, ordinances happen in one of two ways: City staff deems it necessary for the Council to change the law, or City Council asks staff to draft an ordinance for their consideration that would change the law &nbsp;"
